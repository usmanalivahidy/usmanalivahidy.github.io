ENDANGERED ANIMALS

You may be wondering what exactly is an endangered species and why do we need to worry about them so much?

An endangered species is a species that is very likely to become extinct in the near future, either worldwide or in a particular political jurisdiction. Endangered species may be at risk due to factors such as habitat loss, poaching and invasive species. Many nations have laws that protect conservation-reliant species which, for example, forbid hunting, restrict land development, or create protected areas. Some endangered species are the target of extensive conservation efforts such as captive breeding and habitat restoration. Human activity is a significant cause in causing some species to become endangered.

An ecosystem's health is maintained by plants and animals. It's a symptom of an unbalanced environment when a species goes extinct. And the results can be fatal. By reading this article, you can make an impact by learning and raising awareness about endangered species in your area and across the world.
