HOW YOU CAN HELP SAVE THESE WONDERFUL CREATURES

The animals listed have not gone extinct just yet and could still be saved from extinction.

Here is a list of things you as society can do to help these endangered animals and we strongly urge you to do so.
How YOU can help save JAVAN RHINOS
WHAT TO DO WHY THIS WILL HELP
Don't buy rhino horn products Today's greatest threat to rhinos is the illegal trade in rhino horn.
Adopt a rhino When you adopt a rhino, you are preserving the habitat of a threatened rhino species. Your adoption of a rhino ensures that the species can survive in the wild and supports conservation efforts.
Make use of and support sustainable palm oil, paper, and wood. Retailers and manufacturers may help safeguard the habitat of the Sumatran and Javan rhinos by minimising illicit logging and forest conversion by purchasing FSC- and SCS-certified palm oil. Customers may help by only accepting items that are certified.
Donate to WWF To support their work in Africa and Asia on saving these animals.
How YOU can help save AMUR LEOPARDS
WHAT TO DO WHY THIS WILL HELP
Symbolically adopt an Amur leopard The money is used to create anti-poaching units and educate people about the value of the animal across its natural habitat.
Choose sustainable eco-friendly products if you travel to East Asia To help stop the illegal wildlife trade. For instance, you can do this by always asking the vendor where the product came from and what it is made of before purchasing souvenirs
Stick to certified wood products at home, such as those with a Forest Stewardship Council seal So that you may be sure you're not promoting unlawful or unsustainable logging
How YOU can help save SUNDA ISLAND TIGERS
WHAT TO DO WHY THIS WILL HELP
Avoid products containing palm oil or wood Because these are usually havested unsustainably. Instead, look for forest-friendly products that are certified by the Forest Stewardship Council.
Support conservation organizations such as Wildlife Conservation Society Indonesia, and Fauna & Flora International Because these are dedicated to preserving the Sumatran tiger subspecies and supporting them will help the cause

How YOU can help save MOUNTAIN GORILLAS
WHAT TO DO WHY THIS WILL HELP
Ask your politicians to support the Great Ape Conservation Fund going forward and make your opinion known This will provide money and access to additional funds to support a number of field projects around the world
Recycle cell phones and electronics In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, metals are mined from gorilla habitat and utilised in devices like mobile phones. By recycling your mobile phones and other gadgets, you may help conserve gorillas and their environment by lowering the demand for these metals (and the associated hunting that frequently takes place as a result of mining camps in the woods)
Look for sustainable palm oil, wood and other eco-friendly products The harvesting of trees for palm oil, which is used in food and cosmetics, is destroying forest habitats in Asia. A responsible consumer will search for goods that either don't contain palm oil or use palm oil derived from sustainable sources.
How YOU can help save TAPANULI ORANGUTANS
WHAT TO DO WHY THIS WILL HELP
Avoid products that contain unsustainable palm oil Avoiding products that contain palm oil, which is a significant contributor to deforestation, is more crucial than ever for safeguarding the rainforest and its inhabitants
Search for products that bear a Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification Indonesian timber is used to make furniture, cabinets, paper and much more and the unsustainable harvestation of this can destroy orangutan habitat
